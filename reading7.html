A human-made creation that brings out the best in me:

I think that the invention of music is the best out there. It's a basic answer, but I don’t know what people did before being able to listen to music. How did they exercise? How did they do boring tasks? How did they have main-character walks? I am perfectly capable of doing things in silence, but everything is much more enjoyable with music. For example, when I am in the gym walking on the treadmill, it seems like hell when I’m just looking around and hearing the machines and other people’s mumbling conversations. Whereas, when I have my airpods in and a good playlist, I can hype myself up and get lost in it, oftentimes losing track of time– and before you know it, an hour has passed. It can also affect my mood; if I’m feeling down, I can put on a really quick-paced song, or even just one with motivational lyrics, and my thought process just changes completely. It fascinates me that music can affect the brain like that. I don’t know why exactly it has this effect– I may need to do more research on that– but I do know that in the same way it can uplift you, it can also bring you down. I just think that music is an important stimulant for feelings.
