Ritual practice of routine thats imp for psychological well being and fulfilment:

One thing in my routine that really keeps me in check is my alarm system. I have conditioned myself to only wake up once I’ve heard 3 alarms. I will automatically turn off the first two, and then get up on the third. It gives me a little warning without too much of an advance. The alarms need to be exactly 5 minutes apart– no more. 50-55-00. 20-25-30. 35-40-45. This routine ensures that I don’t wake up to anyone else's alarms– only mine. Even if someone’s alarm has the same sound as mine, I won’t wake up because my mind knows I need to hear it 3 times in the 15 minute increment. I’ve been doing this for over 7 years. I don’t wake up from crying babies on planes, I don’t wake up from loud noises on the subway, and I don’t wake up at the crack of dawn from my screaming roommates (who I love so dearly). My brain follows the system consistently, allowing me to get my beauty rest in every situation possible. I feel fulfilled in that I have taken control. It’s nice to be able to know your body and brain so well that you can train it. I don’t know how long it’s taken me to adjust to this, but I know that getting rid of this routine would probably be difficult.
