Longing

Something I long for in the past are the times where I had zero responsibilities– at least not important ones. I yearn for the times I was at camp and cried over what I thought was the end of the world at the time, but truly ended up having no long-term impact. Now, I have real problems and concerns, like grades, getting a job, etc. While I was at camp, I appreciated it. I knew it was something to be grateful for, I just never really anticipated the fact that those years would be my last ones where I could seriously do anything I wanted. Sleeping in had no consequences, skipping a day to do something fun was the norm, and I was lucky enough to have all my friends beside me doing the exact same. I had everything I had ever wanted, I just didn’t realize it at the time. I wish I had more videos, or wrote down journal entries describing my days so that I could look back and reminisce in detail. Eventually, further down the line, those memories will fade. I’m lucky to live in a time where I got to document on my phone, and I do have photos to look back at, but I kinda wish I had more. When I look at them I am reminded of how happy I was and how lucky I was to have these experiences without fearing the future. It’s good that I wasn’t aware– I would have been anxious. It really teaches me how we can be in a moment and think it’s not significant and then look back and it means the world.
