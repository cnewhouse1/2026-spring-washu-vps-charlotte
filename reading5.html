Eno didn’t feel like a typical music documentary, which was interesting to me. I liked that the generative structure made it kind of unpredictable, like the film was actually following Brian Eno’s own creative philosophy. The part where he talks about using oblique strategies to break out of creative ruts really stuck with me- the idea of adding randomness on purpose just feels very freeing. It made creativity seem less serious and intimidating, and more like something you can experiment with. By the end of the documentary, it felt less like a biography and more like spending time inside his mindset.
